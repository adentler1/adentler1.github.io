The Data Visualization Project New

Working paper · A pedagogy for teaching economics through data

A staged semester project in which student teams build data visualizations, evaluate each other against a structured rubric, and iterate until the argument holds. Developed at CIDE over several cohorts, the approach turns data visualization into a vehicle for research design, programming, teamwork, and economic reasoning at once.

Do Private Banks Attempt to Influence Presidential Elections? New

With Enzo Rossi. Accepted, Southern Economic Journal

This paper studies whether private banks adjust lending around presidential elections in ways that are politically meaningful. It puts financial intermediation directly into the electoral process and asks when credit allocation becomes a channel of influence rather than a neutral market outcome.

Residents' Willingness to Pay to Avoid Crime

Journal of Housing Economics, 2024

Crime affects housing markets through more than prices alone. By combining price responses with market liquidity and transaction freezes, the paper estimates welfare losses that are materially larger than standard price-based measures suggest.

Public Debt Management Announcements

Economic Modelling, 2024

Debt managers announce auction volumes before selling sovereign bonds, but actual issuance often departs from those targets. This paper develops a welfare-theoretic explanation for those deviations and shows why credible announcements matter for transparency, learning, and market efficiency.

Monitoring Mexico

An automated monitoring system for Mexican monetary policy and macroeconomic data. The project combines data pipelines, forecasting, visualization, and LLM-based narration to turn daily releases into structured policy updates. Includes @LiquidMexico (data visualization) and Monitoring Minutes (daily AI podcast).
